Robotics and Automation

I've collaborated in a couple of projects with training partners such as ABB, Omron Automation, John Deere, and Acuity Brands

Omron Project (Ciber-Physical systems I)

The main objective proposed by training partner Omron Automation is to integrate their equipment, a TM5M-700 manipulator mounted on an AGV to perform supply and removal of material (micro-logistics) from the Raw Material warehouse to the conveyor belts and Finished Product warehouse. My main involvement in this process is to perform collaborative robot programming of the TM5M-700, creating Pick and Place operations for the removal and manipulation of material, utilizing its 5M pixel integrated camera for image processing, QR Scanning for traceability and robotic visual servoing, color detection for quality control and interfacing using Modbus TCP/IP communication.

ABB Welding Cell

Using CAD tools like SolidWorks, this project involved the design of an automated welding station using an ABB industrial robot, adhering to national and international standards such as: NOM-004-STPS-1999, NOM-018-STPS-2000, NOM-026-STPS-1998 and the ANSI RIA R15.06-1999 to verify the compliance of the proposal with current industry regulations. Validation was done using ABB RobotStudio and Cognex Insight Explorer for the integration of industrial vision systems.

Thrust Test Stand

This project involved the design and development of a thrust test stand to measure the propeller thrust for an aircraft prototype competing at the SAE Aerodesign 2024 carried out in Querétaro, México. I was actively involved in the mechanical design of the test stand, as well as the integration of electronic components to ensure accurate data collection. Using SolidWorks, I designed the entire mechanical structure, focusing on stability, durability, and precision to accommodate the required loads. The system incorporates a load cell and an amplifier module, which were carefully selected and integrated to ensure accurate thrust measurements. The test stand is powered by an ATMEGA328P microcontroller, programmed to capture real-time data from the sensors. This data is processed and stored for subsequent analysis, enabling the evaluation and optimization of the propeller's performance. The project provided valuable experience in combining mechanical design, electronics, and data acquisition for a real-world engineering application.

UR3 Gripper

For the second project in my concentration in Cyber-Physical Systems, I was tasked with developing the initial conceptual design of a gripper capable of picking up metallic clips using an electromagnet and a plastic syringe mounted on it. This gripper allowed the cobot to precisely deposit silicone and insert metallic clips into an LED driver assembly. The mechanical design was created using SolidWorks. To ensure seamless integration between the PCB and the mechanical design, I leveraged the Altium's MCAD CoDesigner add-on. This tool facilitated real-time collaboration between ECAD and MCAD environments, enabling me to verify that the PCB design fit perfectly within the mechanical assembly. The add-on streamlined the design process by allowing quick iteration and validation of changes, ensuring a fully functional and efficient system.

Autonomous Vehicle

In this project, we designed an autonomous vehicle using an STM32 microcontroller. The main focus of this project was the integration of multiple sensor inputs and the development of PID control systems to achieve the desired trajectory to provide a higher level of autonomy for the prototype, recreating the autonomous tractor presented at CES 2022. Analog Circuits
Temperature Control System Using Operational Amplifiers: A Scaled Room Model

In this project for the Analog Electronics course, operational amplifiers were utilized to design and implement a temperature control system for a scaled room model. The process involved circuit design, simulation using LTSpice, and successful prototyping. This project allowed for the practical application of analog circuit concepts, including feedback control and signal processing, while also reinforcing skills in problem-solving and hands-on implementation. The final prototype demonstrated effective temperature regulation, showcasing the reliability and precision of the designed system.

Minisumo Robot
RoboCop

The goal of this project was to put my skills as a mechatronics engineer into practice by designing and developing a robot suitable for the minisumo competition. The main challenge of this project was completing the entire process, from the design phase to prototyping and testing, within a span of just four days. It was a great learning experience, especially considering the limited time available for its development. This project was carried out collaboratively by a team of three students. My primary responsibilities included selecting the actuators and sensors, designing the circuit, and programming the robot to operate autonomously. The electronic circuit was designed using an Arduino Nano, and the development environment used was ArduinoIDE.
